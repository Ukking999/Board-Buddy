Page 1: Introduction to Lines and Angles

Lines and angles are the basic building blocks of geometry.

This chapter covers types of lines, types of angles, pairs of angles formed by transversals, parallel lines properties, and important theorems.

Understanding these concepts is crucial for triangles, quadrilaterals, and circles later.

Page 2: Basic Terms - Point, Line, Ray, Line Segment

  • Point: No size, only position.
  • Line: Infinite in both directions.
  • Ray: Starts at a point, extends infinitely one way.
  • Line Segment: Fixed length between two points.

Collinear points lie on the same line.

Non-collinear do not.

Page 3: Types of Lines

  • Intersecting lines: Meet at one point.
  • Parallel lines: Never meet, same distance apart.
  • Concurrent lines: Three or more lines meeting at one point.

Parallel lines have same direction.

Page 4: Angles - Definition and Types

Angle: Formed by two rays with common endpoint (vertex).

Measured in degrees (¬∞).

Types:

  • Acute: < 90¬∞
  • Right: = 90¬∞
  • Obtuse: > 90¬∞ < 180¬∞
  • Straight: = 180¬∞
  • Reflex: > 180¬∞ < 360¬∞
  • Complete: 360¬∞

Page 5: Pairs of Angles

  • Adjacent angles: Common vertex and arm, no common interior.
  • Complementary: Sum 90¬∞
  • Supplementary: Sum 180¬∞
  • Linear pair: Adjacent + supplementary (on straight line)
Example: If one angle 40¬∞, complementary = 50¬∞, supplementary = 140¬∞.

Page 6: Vertically Opposite Angles

When two lines intersect, opposite angles are equal.

Theorem 6.1: Vertically opposite angles are equal.

Proof: Using adjacent supplementary angles.

Page 7: Transversal and Angles

Transversal: Line cutting two or more lines.

Eight angles formed:

  • Corresponding angles
  • Alternate interior angles
  • Alternate exterior angles
  • Consecutive interior angles

Page 8: Parallel Lines and Transversal

If lines parallel, then:

  • Corresponding angles equal
  • Alternate interior equal
  • Alternate exterior equal
  • Consecutive interior supplementary

Converse also true.

Page 9: Theorem 6.2 and 6.3

6.2: If transversal makes equal corresponding angles ‚Üí lines parallel.
6.3: If transversal makes equal alternate interior angles ‚Üí lines parallel.

Page 10: Theorem 6.4 and 6.5

6.4: Consecutive interior supplementary ‚Üí parallel.
6.5: Lines parallel to same line are parallel to each other.

Page 11: Angle Sum Property

Angles on a straight line = 180¬∞

Angles around a point = 360¬∞

Used frequently in proofs.

Page 12: Key Theorems Summary

  • Vertically opposite equal
  • Corresponding equal ‚áí parallel
  • Alternate interior equal ‚áí parallel
  • Consecutive interior supplementary ‚áí parallel
  • Parallel to same line ‚áí parallel
